Ejection fraction is a measure of how well your heart is pumping blood. It is calculated by dividing the amount of blood pumped out of the heart's left ventricle (the main pumping chamber) by the total amount of blood in the ventricle at the end of diastole (when the heart is relaxed and filling with blood). The result is expressed as a percentage, with a normal ejection fraction ranging from 55% to 70%.
